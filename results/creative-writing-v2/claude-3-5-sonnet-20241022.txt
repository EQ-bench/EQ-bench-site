The ivy on the wall had grown thicker, I noticed, its leaves glossy with health. Perhaps it was time to trim it back, to shape its wild growth into something more contained. Or perhaps – and this thought came with a small smile – I would let it climb, let it seek its own path toward the light.

Tomorrow would come with its own rhythm, its own small ceremonies of living. There would be more books to shelve, more nods to exchange with Mr. Harrison, more cups of tea to brew in the quiet of dawn. But tonight, in this moment between day and darkness, I felt something shift and settle within me, like a book finding its proper place on a shelf. The story wasn't over; it was simply turning to a new chapter, one I would have to write myself.
